This is korpustxt.  Natural language processing uses statistical models to estimate probabilities of words in context.  Simple n gram language models treat each word as a separate symbol in a sequence.  More advanced models use word embeddings and neural networks to capture semantic similarity.  In cognitive science, researchers study how people understand sentences in noise and in natural conversations.  The brain builds hierarchical representations, from sounds and syllables to words, phrases and meanings. Some theories say that the brain constantly predicts the next word and updates its internal model when predictions fail. Even in a small corpus like this, new word types keep appearing as we add more sentences to the text.  This illustrates the idea behind Heaps law, where vocabulary size grows more slowly than the total number of tokens.
